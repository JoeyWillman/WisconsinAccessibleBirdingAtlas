About the Accessible Birding Atlas of Wisconsin

The Accessible Birding Atlas of Wisconsin is a resource dedicated to making birding an inclusive activity for people of all abilities. Our mission is to highlight birding hotspots across Wisconsin that offer accessible trails, facilities, and experiences for everyone.

Our Mission

Our goal is to remove barriers to outdoor recreation by providing detailed information about the accessibility of birding sites. We believe that nature and birding should be accessible to all, regardless of mobility, sensory, or other challenges.

What We Offer

  • Interactive maps highlighting accessible birding locations.
  • Detailed descriptions of site facilities, including parking, trails, and restrooms.
  • A bird checklist generator to help plan your outings based on location and season.
  • Opportunities for users to submit new sites and provide feedback.

Acknowledgments

This project is made possible through collaboration with organizations dedicated to accessibility and conservation, including:

  • Birdability – Promoting inclusive birding experiences.
  • Wisconsin Department of Natural Resources (DNR) – Providing trail and site data.
  • Local Land Trusts and Birding Groups – Supporting the identification and documentation of accessible sites.

Get Involved

We welcome contributions from the birding community! If you know of a site that should be included or have feedback on existing entries, please visit our Submit a Site page. Your input helps us grow and improve this resource.
